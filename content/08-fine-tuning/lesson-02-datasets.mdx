## Plain-English Explanation

Your data is the model's teacher; clean data is safer than more data.

## Key Terms

### Label Noise

Simple meaning:
Wrong or unclear expected outputs.

Real-world analogy:
Teaching from bad examples produces weak employees.

Technical meaning:
Incorrect target pairs that misalign optimization.

Example:
Conflicting response style in two similar prompts.

Why it matters:
Noisy labels create inconsistent behavior and regressions.

## Mental Model

Treat dataset curation as the first model-building step, not a post-task.

## Common Mistakes

- No train/validation split.
- Unbalanced labels.
- Missing edge and rejection samples.
