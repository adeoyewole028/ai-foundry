# What is Fine-Tuning?

## Objective

By the end of this lesson, you can explain where adaptation adds value versus prompt-only solutions.

## Prerequisites

Before this lesson, complete Modules 00–07 and know your product requirements.

## Plain-English Explanation

Fine-tuning adjusts a model's behavior through training examples, often to make it consistently align to a domain.

## Key Terms

### Adaptation

Simple meaning:
Making a model behave the way your product needs.

Real-world analogy:
Personal coaching until a worker consistently follows one style.

Technical meaning:
Parameter updates based on labeled input-output data.

Example:
Standardizing style and policy language for internal assistants.

Why it matters:
Adaptation can improve consistency and reduce prompt complexity.

## Mental Model

Think of base capability as foundation and fine-tuning as specialized finishing.

## Example

A legal chatbot that follows your office tone while handling policy questions.

## Code Walkthrough

No code required. Document why adaptation is the right lever and the expected tradeoffs.

## Common Mistakes

- Using fine-tuning where prompt improvements are enough.
- Ignoring data quality as first-class risk.
- No measurable baseline for comparison.
