# AI Foundations Quiz

## Objective

By the end of this quiz, you will be able to check whether you can explain the full AI Foundations module in plain English.

## Prerequisites

Before this quiz, you should complete all ten AI Foundations lessons.

## Plain-English Explanation

This quiz is not about memorizing perfect definitions. It is about proving that you can explain AI concepts clearly enough to make product decisions. Answer in your own words and use concrete examples from GigSwipe, FurOrbit, ResQ, or a product you know well.

## Key Terms

### Concept Check

A concept check asks whether you can explain an idea and apply it to a realistic situation.

### Product Reasoning

Product reasoning means connecting a technical idea to user value, cost, risk, and workflow fit.

## Mental Model

Imagine explaining the module to a founder who wants to add AI to a product next week. Your job is to be clear, practical, and honest about tradeoffs.

## Example

A strong answer does not just say, "RAG uses documents." It says, "RAG retrieves relevant documents and gives them to an LLM so the answer can be grounded in current product or policy information."

## Code Walkthrough

No code is required for this quiz. If a question mentions a system, describe the flow in steps.

## Common Mistakes

- Repeating a term without explaining it.
- Saying AI is always better than rules.
- Ignoring safety, evaluation, and user workflow.
- Treating a model as the whole product.

## Exercise

Answer each question in complete sentences:

1. What is AI in practical software terms?
2. When should a product use exact rules instead of AI?
3. What is the relationship between machine learning, deep learning, and LLMs?
4. What is a model, and how is it different from the app around it?
5. What are tokens, and why do token limits matter?
6. What are embeddings, and how do they enable semantic search?
7. What is RAG, and when is it useful?
8. What makes an AI agent different from a chatbot?
9. How would you decide whether an AI feature is worth building?
10. Name two AI safety risks and one guardrail for each.

## Reflection

Which concept changed how you think about building software with AI, and why?
