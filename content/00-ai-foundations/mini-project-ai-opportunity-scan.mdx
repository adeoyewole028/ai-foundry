# Mini Project: AI Opportunity Scan

## Objective

By the end of this mini project, you will be able to inspect a real workflow and decide where AI might help, where it should not be used, and what evidence would prove value.

## Prerequisites

Before this mini project, you should complete the ten AI Foundations lessons and the quiz.

## Plain-English Explanation

An AI opportunity scan is a lightweight audit. You choose a workflow, look for friction, and decide whether AI is a good fit. This prevents the common mistake of adding AI because it sounds impressive instead of because it solves a real problem.

## Key Terms

### Workflow

A workflow is the sequence of steps a person or team follows to get something done.

### Opportunity

An opportunity is a place where AI could reduce effort, improve quality, speed up decisions, or help users handle complexity.

### Constraint

A constraint is a reason the AI idea may be unsafe, too expensive, too slow, too unreliable, or unnecessary.

## Mental Model

Think like a product detective. You are looking for a useful opening, but you are also looking for reasons to say no.

## Example

Workflow: ResQ dispatchers read incident reports and route breakdown requests.

Possible AI opportunity: classify urgency and draft a dispatcher summary.

Possible non-AI alternative: add required form fields and rule-based priority tags.

Risk: a model may miss urgent context or overstate confidence.

Success signal: dispatchers route incidents faster without increasing misroutes.

## Code Walkthrough

No code is required. Use this structure:

```txt
Workflow -> Friction -> AI opportunity -> Non-AI alternative -> Risk -> Success signal
```

## Common Mistakes

- Picking a workflow you do not understand.
- Ignoring a simple non-AI solution.
- Choosing a metric that only measures usage.
- Forgetting safety or review steps.

## Exercise

Choose one workflow from your work, school, or a product you use. Write:

1. The workflow.
2. The person doing the work.
3. The painful step.
4. One AI opportunity.
5. One non-AI alternative.
6. One risk.
7. One success signal.

## Reflection

What would make AI the wrong tool for this workflow?
