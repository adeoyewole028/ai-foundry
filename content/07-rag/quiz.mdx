## Key Terms

### Retrieval budget

Simple meaning:
How much retrieval cost and context you can spend.

Real-world analogy:
How much time to research before responding.

Technical meaning:
Top-k, rerank depth, and context window allocation.

Example:
Choosing k=6 for complex query and k=3 for simple query.

Why it matters:
Budget tradeoffs affect speed, cost, and answer quality.
