## Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will evaluate models with task-appropriate metrics.

## Prerequisites

Before this lesson, you should complete classification and train/validation workflows.

## Plain-English Explanation

Accuracy says “how often we’re right,” but it is not always the right scoreboard.

## Key Terms

### Precision

Simple meaning:
When we predict positive, how often we are correct.

Real-world analogy:
Calling someone for interview; precision is keeping false positives low.

Technical meaning:
TP / (TP + FP) for positive predictions.

Example:
Fraud detection with many legitimate transactions.

Why it matters:
Wrong metric choice can hide harmful errors.

## Mental Model

Use a scoreboard that matches your business pain, not just convenience.

## Example

If missing a fraud is costly, you prioritize recall for fraud cases.

## Code Walkthrough

Generate and inspect confusion matrix plus precision/recall, then choose a threshold from risk.

## Common Mistakes

- Celebrating high accuracy when positives are rare.
- Treating recall and precision as interchangeable.
- Tuning metrics only on test data.
