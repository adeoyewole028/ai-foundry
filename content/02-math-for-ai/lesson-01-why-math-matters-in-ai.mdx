AI needs math because computers cannot directly understand resumes, images, documents, or user intent. They need numbers that stand in for those things.

## Key Terms

### Measurement

Simple meaning:
Turning something into numbers.

Real-world analogy:
Using a thermometer to turn "hot" into `32 C`.

Technical meaning:
A numeric representation of a property, signal, feature, or behavior.

Example:
A spam detector may measure message length, suspicious words, and sender history.

Why it matters:
AI systems need measurements before they can compare examples or learn patterns.

## Mental Model

Math is the dashboard under the AI product. Users see recommendations, answers, and predictions. Underneath, the system is measuring inputs, comparing options, and adjusting mistakes.

## Example

A resume analyzer might convert a job post and resume into numbers, compare them, then produce a match score.

## Code Walkthrough

```python
resume_skills = 7
required_skills = 10

match_score = resume_skills / required_skills
print(match_score)
```

This is not a full AI system, but it shows the pattern: turn a situation into numbers, then make a decision from the numbers.

## Common Mistakes

- Thinking math is the goal instead of a tool.
- Treating AI scores as perfect truth.
- Memorizing formulas before understanding what the system is measuring.
