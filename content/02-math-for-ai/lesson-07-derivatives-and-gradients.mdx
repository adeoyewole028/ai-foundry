## Prerequisites

Before this lesson, you should understand that model training tries to reduce mistakes.

## Plain-English Explanation

A derivative tells you how something changes. A gradient tells a model which direction to move to improve.

You do not need to solve calculus problems right now. You need the product intuition: gradients are signals that point toward lower error.

## Key Terms

### Gradient

Simple meaning:
Direction of improvement.

Real-world analogy:
Walking downhill in fog by feeling which way the ground slopes.

Technical meaning:
A collection of partial derivatives that indicates the direction of steepest increase for a function.

Example:
During training, a gradient tells the model how to adjust weights to reduce loss.

Why it matters:
Models need directional feedback to improve step by step.

## Mental Model

Picture a valley. The model is standing somewhere on a hill of mistakes. The gradient tells it which way the slope points.

## Example

If changing a model weight makes error go down, the gradient helps the training process keep moving in that useful direction.

## Code Walkthrough

```python
current_error = 10
next_error = 7

change = next_error - current_error
print(change)
```

A negative change means the error went down. Real gradients calculate this direction more carefully across many weights.

## Common Mistakes

- Thinking gradients are only abstract calculus.
- Forgetting that gradients are used to update model weights.
- Confusing direction with the final answer.
