## Plain-English Explanation

Probability is a way to talk about uncertainty. It helps AI systems say, "This outcome is more likely than that one."

AI rarely knows the future with certainty. It estimates.

## Key Terms

### Probability

Simple meaning:
How likely something is.

Real-world analogy:
A weather app saying there is a 70% chance of rain.

Technical meaning:
A value between 0 and 1 that represents likelihood.

Example:
A classifier may estimate that an email has `0.92` probability of being spam.

Why it matters:
AI products need to handle uncertainty honestly.

## Mental Model

Probability is a confidence dial, not a truth stamp.

## Example

A support triage model might predict `billing`, `technical`, or `account` as possible ticket categories and choose the most likely one.

## Code Walkthrough

```python
probabilities = {
    "billing": 0.65,
    "technical": 0.25,
    "account": 0.10,
}

prediction = max(probabilities, key=probabilities.get)
print(prediction)
```

The selected label is the strongest guess, not a guarantee.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating probability as certainty.
- Ignoring low-probability but high-risk outcomes.
- Showing confidence scores to users without explaining what they mean.
