## Key Terms

### Reliability envelope

Simple meaning:
The range where your system behaves predictably.

Real-world analogy:
Running features behind known guardrails before full release.

Technical meaning:
Defined quality, latency, and safety boundaries for each flow.

Example:
Reject low-confidence outputs and send them for human review.

Why it matters:
Reliable systems outperform clever prompts when failure cost is high.
